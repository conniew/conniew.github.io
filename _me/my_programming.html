2004

I unknowingly began experimenting with web development, editing premade templates for character pages. I may have used a marquee or two. Sorry.

2011

I sat down to a COS126 lecture at Princeton University for the first time. The world of programming opened up like a jQuery accordion effect.

2013

I interned at Epic Systems and discovered Wisconsin cheese curds. They would forever perpetuate the web app I created for drawing and annotating heart defects.

2014

I graduated with a B.S. in computer science and began full-time front-end work on Google Cloud Platform. I also began hiding from any relatives of mine that used Google products.

2015

I decided to build a personal website. I promise I won't use any marquees.

2019

I went meta and joined the front-end team working on Google Chrome. Now I develop for the web in more ways than one.




Languages and Constructs (decreasing familiarity): Javascript, HTML/CSS, C++, MATLAB, Python, Java, C, English.
