--- layout: permalink date: "2020-01-02" title: "Pool Party" subtitle: "short story, character" ---

Earth sank in up to her waist and sighed. She savored the tingling sensation as her legs and feet dissolved into mud and floated out into the pool. The water was cool, but it would warm up quickly as her mud soaked up the Sun.

She felt a tremble in the water, but she tried to ignore it. She lowered herself a bit further and let her mud settle, enjoying the grittiness of being silt. But the trembling continued, until it finally erupted. Her mud stirred violently and was sucked into a whirlpool. It pulled her down to her chest, and her torso began to dissolve.

While she still had the lungs to yell, she roared, "Water!"

Water poked her head up and giggled. Then she dropped back in, blending seamlessly into the pool. Earth tried to solidify, but Water became a whirling torpedo and started scattering her mud for fun. She sailed by faster than Earth could follow, then leapt out entirely, glittering in the Sun and shedding raindrops into the water. Then she disappeared without a splash and started again, stirring up dozens of tiny whirlpools. Soon, the pool was completely brown with Earth's mud, interrupted only by Water's clear form making figure eights under the surface.

Grumbling at the disturbance, Earth submerged all the way and saturated the pool with mud. As the water got thicker, she could feel Water's movement becoming sluggish. Dragging her mud along, Earth lumbered after Water through the swirling muck, trying to condense enough to trap her.

But suddenly Earth's mud began to churn and sputter. Water squealed in glee and rippled to the surface, reveling in the new sensation. Air was blowing bubbles into the pool. She drifted idly above them, floating effortlessly on the water. Everywhere her body touched, the water frothed vigorously, making it impossible for Earth to thicken her mud.

White foam collected on the surface, blanketing the pool. Water delighted in cresting in and out, gathering big armfuls of foam and watching it dissolve back into the water. Every once in a while, she grabbed Air's leg and tried to dunk her in the pool. Each time, Air bobbed back up in a flurry of bubbles and went back to drifting on the surface.

With Water distracted, Earth returned to relaxing. She gathered her mud loosely around her and swam lazily underwater. As she rocked back and forth, she could feel her mud trailing slightly behind her. It felt good not to be so solid for once.

"Stop splashing!"

The complaint silenced Air's bubbles and Water's squeals. Earth poked her head above the water and shuddered. Fire stood at the edge of the pool, glowering down at all of them. Her entire left shoulder smoldered angrily, spitting steam into the air. Earth looked at Water, who looked at Air. Somebody was in big trouble.

"Uh oh," Air whispered. Earth trembled and sank out of sight.

Fire flared as bright as the Sun and surged forward, stepping straight onto Air to avoid touching the water. Air squirmed in protest, but Fire ignored her and leapt straight for Water. Frozen in fear, Water reacted too slowly. Fire caught her ankle as she tried to dive, stopping her from going under.

Fire's hand and Water's foot dissolved simultaneously in a hiss of steam. Fire reached again with her other hand, but Water managed to dip under the surface. She fled down to the bottom of the pool, where Earth had settled in a heap. Stirring at the disruption, Earth opened up a hole in her mud and offered a place to hide. Water burrowed in and curled into a ball, whimpering in distress.

On the surface, Air blew Fire aside until they both alighted safely at the edge of the pool. Then she shrouded Fire's hand in her own, breathing onto it to help it rekindle faster. She did the same for her shoulder, and the hissing steam faded to a soft crackle. Fire's angry flames dimmed reluctantly.

Earth slowly solidified out of the pool, gathering her mud into clumps. Once her hands were formed, she cupped some water into the shape of a foot and coaxed it onto Water's ankle. Then she gently drew Water from the pool, patting her back into shape whenever she tried to melt back in.

Back in their regular forms, the four of them stood silently at the edge of the pool. Then Water abruptly vaporized and cried, "Race you to the Sun!"

Earth grumbled restlessly as she watched the others take off into the sky.
